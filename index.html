The SociAbility Project aims to create a digital platform for people with disability in Australia.

Project Summary

Our goal is to build social inclusion, reduce loneliness and create a sense of community.

What is sociability? The Cambridge Dictionary defines sociability as the quality of liking to meet and spend time with other people.

We want to see people with and without disability spending time together as friends and equals.

“Diversity is being invited to the party and inclusion is being asked to dance” - Verna Myers

So . . . shall we dance?

Who's it for?

Anyone who is feeling a bit lonely, who wants to find genuine friendship and have a more meaningful life.

Who's involved?

We’re a small group of people throughout Australia with disabilities and health issues who want to help similar people connect with each other and their communities.

Research

Research shows that making friends is difficult for people with a disability. This is an international problem.

People with a disability are among society’s most excluded. They have among the highest rates of employment and lowest rates of social participation.

We want to change that by removing the social and physical barriers.

How research helps

We’ve curated research that explores the problems and solutions for connecting people with and without a disability.

We also do our own research. There are 3 areas we’ve been exploring to understand the extent of the problem and what the SociAbility Project can work on.

Get Involved

How you can get involved?

We seek your interest and support to help more people live richer lives and find people they can genuinely connect with.

Interested in being more inclusive?

Check out the research we’ve curated to learn more. Or go to resources for practical tips on how to hold inclusive and accessible events. Resources also has information about how interact respectfully with people with disabilities.

Looking for activities or places that are inclusive and disability-friendly?

Go to our resources section to see if there’s anything near you. You’ll find fact sheets, checklists, and listings.

Want to volunteer with the SociAbility Project?

We are a small group of volunteers, mostly with disabilities and chronic illness. We’re always all on the lookout for people with skills to work with us.
